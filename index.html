Introduction

Nick Drake was an English folk singer/songwriter whose enduring and enchanting music is cited to this day as a major influence by musicians from a broad array of genres. Virtually unknown in his lifetime, his music gained the cult status it still enjoys today only after his tragic death in 1974 aged just 26 as a result of profound and chronic mental illness.

Studio albums

Nick Drake's music career spanned just five years from 1969 to 1974. In this time he produced three impressive but hugely contrasting studio albums. From the bright, optimistic and richly-produced Five leaves left, to the stark, pared-down sound and at times bitterly self-critical lyrics of Pink Moon, Drake's music tells the story of his mental decline and suffering.
